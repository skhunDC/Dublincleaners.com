PRE-WEDDING FAQS

Where/how should I be storing my dress leading up to the wedding? If you are having your gown cleaned months before your first alterations appointment, please keep the gown in the cloth bag you received from your bridal boutique, zipped in a closet in a temperature-controlled room. Do not store in a basement, attic or hot room. You should be sure the gown bag is not exposed to natural light. You should also be sure your gown bag is not accessible to house pets or small children.

When you get home hang it as high as possible with nothing in contact at the top. Sweep the back and train out just as if it were going down the aisle. Also before the wedding hang your dress so it’s not exposed to direct sunlight and also where the dress will not be accessible to small children or house pets.

How to transport your gown from Dublin Cleaners after pre-wedding pressing? Our staff will assist you in putting the gown into your vehicle. We recommend having the gown picked up in a vehicle where the back seats can lay flat. The gown bag will stay secured in place by placing the hanger hook on the outside head-rest post on the passenger side of your vehicle. Plan to have someone at the destination where you are taking it to meet you and assist you in taking it into the home or venue. Do not do errands once you pick up your gown. Go directly to where you are storing it, especially in hot months. Leaving it in the vehicle might cause it to wrinkle in some areas.

Suggestions for avoiding stains and damage: Especially in warm weather months, avoid the bottom of your gown and train to be exposed to parking lots. Walking on sidewalks can also cause abrasions on the bottom of your gown. Also, do not allow your gown to be near black mulch beds as the dye in the mulch can transfer to the bottom of your gown.

Post‑Wedding FAQs

Do I need an appointment?

No. Visit our main location (6845 Caine Road, Columbus, OH 43235). In Licking County, you may use either Granville Cleaners location. The initial inspection/paperwork takes ~20 minutes.

What about tough stains?

Bring your gown ASAP for a free consultation. We’ll assess realistic outcomes. “Invisible stains” (champagne, icing, clear beverages, perspiration) can oxidize and yellow—clean your gown even if it appears clean.

Can I open the preservation box?

You can view your gown through the chest window. We do not seal the chest and provide white cotton gloves to gently recenter the bodice if it shifts. Removing the gown requires re‑cleaning before returning to the chest.

What are your guarantees?

We advise how/where to store your acid‑free chest (temperature‑controlled, never plastic, not in basement/attic/hot rooms/garage/crawl space/storage). As Central Ohio’s only Association of Wedding Gown Specialists member, your guarantee is honored worldwide.

How long does preservation take?

We handle 1,000+ gowns/year. Turnaround is typically 6–10 weeks (Dec–Apr is fastest; May–Nov is busiest). Have a special date? Ask—we often can accommodate with a month’s notice.

Tips for the Bride

Wedding Gown Fittings

Bring your wedding shoes to every fitting; wear the same undergarments for consistency.

Hanging Your Gown

Use inside loops attached to sturdy seams; never hang by shoulders/straps. Keep the hem off the floor.

Inspection

Check for loose threads, beads, buttons, seams, and crushed areas a few days before the wedding. Need a refreshing press? Call us.

Garment Bag

On the day, remove the gown from the bag and fluff layers. Don’t iron/steam yourself to avoid water stains. Our pressed gowns come in a protective, billowy white bag—ready to step into.

Hair & Makeup

Cover your front with a sheet; wear a button‑down/robe while you finish hair and makeup.

Caring for Your Gown

Stand when possible; if you sit, use a backless stool/chair to keep fullness and avoid wrinkles.

Getting to the Reception

Lay the train across the seat next to you; sit forward to avoid wrinkling. Watch doors, puddles, mud, and newly sealed blacktop.

Photos

Use a white pillowcase/towel underfoot outside. Avoid black mulch and fresh driveway sealer—two of the hardest stains to remove.

No Sparklers

We’ve seen irreparable sparkler damage—consider other send‑off options.

Storage Afterward

Avoid plastic (emits fumes and traps moisture). Contact us for cleaning & preservation.

Cleaning Matters

“Invisible stains” (champagne, icing, perspiration, clear beverages) oxidize and yellow—clean even if it looks spotless.
